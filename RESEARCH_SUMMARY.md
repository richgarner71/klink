# Kink/Fetish Community Resources Research - Summary Report

**Research Focus**: North America  
**Date Completed**: November 20, 2025  
**Total Resources Cataloged**: 31

---

## Executive Summary

This research identifies existing resources in the kink/fetish community across North America to assess the need for an AI-based safety tool or service. The catalog demonstrates that while educational resources exist, there is **significant opportunity** for a comprehensive, AI-powered safety and planning tool that could:

1. Consolidate fragmented information across multiple platforms
2. Provide personalized risk assessment and safety guidance
3. Offer interactive scene planning with safety checks
4. Deliver real-time consent and negotiation support
5. Create a centralized database of safety protocols and best practices

## Research Findings by Category

### Category 1: Educational Resources, Databases, or LLMs (10 resources)

**Key Finding**: While educational platforms exist, **NO comprehensive AI tool or LLM specifically designed for kink safety planning was found**.

**Identified Resources**:
- **Risk Evaluation Database (RED)** - Static database, not AI-powered
- **Kink Academy** - Video education platform (2,000+ videos)
- **Dom Sub Living** - Training courses and guides
- **The Duchy** - Rope bondage specific education
- **Submissive Guide** - 15+ years of educational content
- Others: SADE, Kink Knowledgeable Program, OhYesPlease, Unearthed Pleasures, BDSM Wiki

**Gap Identified**: Existing resources are primarily:
- Static educational content (videos, articles, guides)
- Not interactive or personalized
- Require users to piece together information from multiple sources
- No AI-powered risk assessment or scene planning tools
- No dynamic consent documentation systems

## Market Gap Analysis: Opportunity for AI-Based Tool

### Current Landscape Limitations

1. **Information Fragmentation**
   - Users must visit 10+ websites/apps to gather comprehensive safety information
   - No single source of truth for kink safety practices
   - Inconsistent terminology and approaches across resources

2. **Lack of Personalization**
   - Static content doesn't adapt to individual risk profiles
   - No consideration of experience level, medical conditions, or specific kinks
   - One-size-fits-all approach to safety guidance

3. **Limited Interactivity**
   - Most resources are passive (videos, articles)
   - No real-time Q&A or scenario-specific guidance
   - Checklists exist but lack intelligence or context

4. **No Comprehensive Scene Planning**
   - Existing apps focus on single aspects (checklists, habit tracking)
   - No end-to-end scene planning with integrated safety checks
   - Missing risk assessment and mitigation recommendations

5. **Consent Documentation Gaps**
   - Limited tools for documenting consent and negotiation
   - No standardized formats or best practices
   - Difficult to track evolving boundaries and agreements

### Proposed AI Tool Value Proposition

An AI-based kink safety tool could provide:

**Core Features**:
1. **Intelligent Risk Assessment**
   - Personalized risk evaluation based on activities, experience, health factors
   - Real-time safety alerts and recommendations
   - Context-aware guidance for specific scenarios

2. **Interactive Scene Planning**
   - Step-by-step planning with safety checkpoints
   - Equipment recommendations and safety protocols
   - Emergency planning and aftercare guidance

3. **Comprehensive Knowledge Base**
   - Consolidates information from all existing resources
   - AI-powered search and recommendations
   - Continuously updated with community input

4. **Consent & Negotiation Support**
   - Guided negotiation framework
   - Digital consent documentation
   - Communication templates and prompts

5. **Community Safety Features**
   - Anonymous reporting of safety concerns
   - Vetted professional directory integration
   - Connection to local munches and educational events

**Target Users**:
- Newcomers seeking comprehensive safety guidance
- Experienced practitioners wanting structured planning tools
- Couples/groups needing communication and documentation support
- Educators and workshop facilitators
- Kink-aware professionals

## Additional Observations

### Safety Principles Emphasized Across All Resources
- **Consent**: Explicit, enthusiastic, informed, and ongoing
- **Communication**: Pre-scene negotiation, check-ins, aftercare
- **Risk Awareness**: Understanding and accepting potential risks (RACK)
- **Boundaries**: Hard limits vs. soft limits, respect for boundaries
- **Safe Words**: Traffic light system (Red/Yellow/Green) widely adopted
- **Vetting**: Importance of community vetting and references

### Geographic Coverage
All resources identified serve the North American market, with particular strength in:
- United States (East Coast and West Coast focus)
- Canada (limited specific resources but included in broader platforms)
- Strong presence of in-person events in major cities (NYC, Chicago, San Francisco, LA)

### Community Structure
- FetLife serves as primary social network (1M+ members)
- Munches are key entry point for newcomers
- Educational events (Kinky Kollege, conferences) provide hands-on learning
- Kink-aware professionals directory critical for non-pathologizing support

## Conclusion

This research reveals a **substantial opportunity** for an AI-based kink safety tool that could significantly improve safety outcomes in the community. While existing resources provide valuable education, they lack the intelligence, personalization, and integration necessary for comprehensive safety planning. A well-designed AI tool addressing these gaps would fill a critical need and likely see strong adoption from a community that prioritizes safety and informed consent.
